What is Domestication Antiquity?

Domestication antiquity is the estimated number of years before present when a wild plant species was first domesticated — meaning humans began selecting it for cultivation and altered its genetics through that process.

These are minimum estimates based on the earliest written records, archaeological evidence, or genetic data available in the literature.

Data Coverage
210 crops have antiquity data

Only 210 of 867 crops (24%) in the database have a recorded domestication date. The remaining 76% have no known estimate in the published literature.

Data Quality

The quality of antiquity records varies greatly — from single references in grey literature to well-established results from multiple genomic and archaeological studies. All values should be treated as minimum estimates subject to revision.
